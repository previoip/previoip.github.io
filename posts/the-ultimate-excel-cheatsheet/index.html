The Ultimate Excel Cheatsheet

Another day another generic HR engagement-bait post of someones supposedly excel shortcuts cheatsheet; on paper, flourish typeface, listing some of the most basic, downright obvious, Microsoft Excel shortcuts.

I’m just one random white-collar wagie, but whenever I see reposts of a shallow-dive of the word shortcut, I got triggered.

Sure I only have less than 2 years of experience, but having 5-6 hours staring at multiple sheets daily, and at times I need to revise reports last-SECOND to deadline, I feel like I need to interject.


Shortcut - A Manifesto

I’d say shortcut is more than just assigned key to sets of action to be readily available on your keyboard-press. It can be game changer to the way you do things.

One prime example is U in after effects, which upon trigger it exposes active keyframes on selected object in timeline editor, and double click to toggle for all objects. Simple yet could boost productivity tenfold. Instead of having to check hundreds of objects and properties manually, you can easily show all already keyframed entities.

Or more nuanced shortcut like holding shift when dragging shapes on powerpoint to allow only lateral movement, or ctrl to disable snapping to other object. And so on and so forth.

These two examples are hidden in their respective user experience. One is exactly a macro as we previously described, the other one is more of a feature.

In practice you don’t need to know all these things. More likely you’ll find yourself in situation where you need to optimize things a bit and wham, its muscle memory all of the sudden.

the frills of dopamine while swiftly traversing and manipulating the work in front of you in fraction of a second.
